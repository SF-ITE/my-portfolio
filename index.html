Block Ninja

A little casual game I made in .js that is, for legal reasons, quite completely dissimilar to any other “Ninja” game that may have been popular ten years ago. Slash the blocks with mouse and left button. Destroying special blocks slows down time for a bit.
The app is live at: https://ericerrickson.ml/blockninja

Eric's Cube

With heartfelt apologies to Ernő Rubik, may I present a new take on a classic, the Eric’s Cube. I put a lot of effort into making it responsive enough to feel like a native app on mobile. You tell me. There are plenty of options to explore under settings. Try a 5x5 cube!
The app is live at: https://ericscube.ml

Web Toys

The result of a coding competition a couple of years ago, the web toys comprise a playable blues guitar in .js, some interactive dancing robots, a real time cryptocurrency tracker, and an eight bit version of Grand Theft Auto set in 1986. Feel free to modify my code as you wish and play around to your heart’s content.
The app is live at: https://errickson.000webhostapp.com/noodles/

The E.R.I.C. Platform

I built the Electronic Retail Integration Console for a startup that had grown too fast to continue filling and shipping orders with a groaning Access database and thousands of Post-Its. It is now three years old and deprecated, but I still get the warm nostalgia buzz when I see it. I may rebuild it with modern tools at some stage.
A demo instance of the app is live at: ericerrickson.ml/eric/
